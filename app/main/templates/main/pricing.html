T-Shirt Quilt Preparation

  1. Gather shirts you want to use making a pile of "must be used" in the quilt and a small pile of "can be used" to fill in areas.
  2. Place a piece of painter's tape 3-6 inches long on top of graphic to be used.
  3. Decide what style quilt you want from the 3 above. Also decide if you want to add a border to the Style 1 or 3.
  4. Shirts with graphics on front and back to be used are considered 2 shirts. Small pocket size graphics on the front of the shirt and/ or sleeve can be combined to make one block in any style. (To be charged at half shirt price per graphic.)
  5. Decide on whether you will provide backing or have The Quilting Lilly (TQL) provide for a fee (typically $20yd).

Just Quilting Services Preparation

  1. Quilt top: Pressed with all seams pressed and laying flat either to side or open. Please trim excess threads.
  2. Backing: Pressed and seam/s pressed flat either to side or open.
  3. Backing: At least 4 inches wider than quilt top on each side, top, and bottom. Sheets are not a good choice for backing. Please discuss with me before choosing sheeting for backing.
  4. Batting: At least 3-4 inches wider than quilt top on each side, top, and bottom. You can get batting from me. If you choose to provide batting, please remove batting from package and lay out to relax a day or two to remove creases before bringing to me. You may also need to press/steam out deep creases.
